The Morgan Stanley Offer

Austin, TX
November 29, 1996
Dear Dad and Mom, I received both of your letters a while ago, but I just haven’t been able to find the time to write back. I’m so sorry. I've been busy with one thing after another, although sometimes I'm not even sure what I've been so busy doing. I heard that Mom has officially retired! I can't believe how quickly that happened. It’s actually great news, though; now you don't have to stress over teaching classes and grading assignments anymore, and you can finally just enjoy yourself. Dad, you still have a long way to go until retirement, right? I’ve had quite a few interviews over the past month. I took a trip out to New York for one, but otherwise, they've mostly been right here in Austin. Many companies have been coming to campus to recruit. I actually went out and bought a suit specifically for two second-round interviews—it cost around $320, and I’ve only worn it twice so far! Both of those companies ended up extending me job offers. One is National Instruments here in Austin, a smaller company with about 1,000 employees that mostly makes PC hardware and software. They offered a base salary of $42,000 plus an 8–9% annual bonus, which brings it to an estimated $46,000 a year. The other offer is from Morgan Stanley, a big and highly prestigious financial firm that specializes in wealth management and investment banking. My trip to New York was actually for their on-site interview. They offered a total compensation package of $62,500, which includes a $50,000 base salary plus a guaranteed minimum bonus of $12,500. It also comes with four weeks of paid vacation every year. While that looks like a huge number on paper, the job is located on Broadway in Manhattan, and the cost of living there is much higher than elsewhere, so the salary isn't quite as high as it seems. Between the two, I’m leaning toward Morgan Stanley. They were actually my top choice going into this semester, so I’m thrilled that I landed the offer. If I take the Morgan Stanley job, I’ll probably live in New Jersey and commute into New York City every day. If I were to live in the city, there'd be no way for me to keep a car because parking is virtually nonexistent. Either way, things are looking up! I plan to keep looking next semester and see what I can find in California. I’ve heard California is great, though the cost of living is very high there too. As for the car, I haven't had much chance to practice driving because everyone is busy—I'm busy, and my friends are busy too—so it's hard to find time that works for everyone. I've only practiced three times so far. The first two lessons were with a Taiwanese friend, and Robin taught me once as well. I can manage the basics fine, but obviously, I still need a lot of practice. I’ll keep working on it over winter break. I promise I’ll be careful. Our winter break runs from mid-December to January 12th. I decided against going back to work at Bell Labs this winter; the break is too short, and I don't really want to spend the holidays working anyway. I’d rather just relax and recharge here in Austin. I had originally thought about coming home, but when I called the travel agency a month in advance, they told me all the flights were completely booked up, so I dropped the idea. I guess I'll just wait for you to come and visit me instead. Honestly, anytime works, so just get everything ready on your end. Weihai found a job too! He's going to Microsoft with a starting base salary of $47,000, which is quite good. He graduates at the end of this year. Interestingly, several of my classmates from Beijing who came to UT for their Ph.D. programs have decided to drop out and just look for jobs instead. Part of it is because the job market is so hot right now, and the other part is that a Ph.D. just takes up way too much time. I'm doing really well here in the States. Even though I’ve been incredibly busy lately, I'm in good health; I've been here for over a year now and have only felt under the weather once. My roommate is transferring soon and moving near Dallas, so I'll also be moving in mid-December and renting a room from a Taiwanese friend. Once I’ve moved, I’ll send over my new address and phone number. I'll stop here for now. Wishing you both good health, Tong 11/28/1996 P.S. Today is Thanksgiving—the American holiday where everyone eats turkey.
