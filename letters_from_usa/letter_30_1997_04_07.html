The Swans of Town Lake

Austin, TX
April 7, 1997
Dear Dad and Mom, I received your letter dated March 12th. I assume Mom is out in Chongming right now? Today happens to be the Qingming Festival. Things always seem so complicated on Mom's side of the family; honestly, if she doesn't really want to go, she shouldn't worry about what anyone else thinks. I’m officially graduating in a little over a month. I’ve already taken care of everything I need to do beforehand—like applying for graduation and filing for my work permit (OPT). These things are just standard administrative procedures, so there shouldn't be any issues. Graduation is around mid-May (the 15th or so), but I think I should be completely done with everything by early May. That said, I don’t plan on heading out to New York/New Jersey right away. For one thing, I need to wait for my actual diploma and work permit to arrive, and plus, I have a lot of friends here in Austin, so it's a bit more fun. I think I’ll wait until mid-to-late June before making the move. I don’t really want to sell my car, so I'm planning to drive it all the way out there—which means driving across half the country. It would be ideal to find someone to make the road trip with me to make it more interesting. I’ll probably also go on a trip with some friends sometime in mid-to-late May. Finding an apartment in NY/NJ is going to be a hassle; I might ask a friend to help me look, or just crash at a friend’s place when I get there and look for a place myself. Everything will work out in the end, so please don't stress over it. Once I start working, the green card process will probably be on the slower side and take a few years. Because of that, the only real option is for you to come and visit me. I think next year would be the perfect time. By then, I’ll be completely settled into my new environment, money won't be an issue, and I’ll be able to take some time off to show you around. Please don't plan on bringing your own money to spend in America—that makes absolutely no financial sense. Any expenses related to your visit will be my responsibility. I’m enclosing some camera negatives with this letter, mostly photos of me and Sue. Sue is a friend I met through the International Office's PALS program (Partnerships to Advance Language Study). She’s in her late 40s, and we meet once a week to do a language and culture exchange. She just got back from Japan recently and has actually traveled to China before, including Nanjing. She is a incredibly nice lady. Her father was a professor here at UT who passed away not too long ago. Sue is originally German but grew up in the States, and she lived in Japan for five or six years. The photos were all taken here in Austin—some are at the State Capitol, and others are by the water at Town Lake. (I included a few shots of a swan; this swan was hilarious and so greedy for food that it almost swallowed my finger!) All in all, Austin really is a wonderful city. You definitely won’t find any swans like that in New York City. Love, Tong 4/5/97
