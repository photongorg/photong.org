The Swans of Town Lake

Austin, TX
April 7, 1997
Dear Dad and Mom, I received your letter dated March 12th. Today happens to be the Qingming Festival. I assume Mom is already out in Chongming right now? Things always seem so complicated on Mom's side of the family; honestly, if she doesn't really want to go, she shouldn't worry about what anyone else thinks. I’m officially graduating in a little over a month. I’ve already taken care of everything I need to do beforehand—like applying for graduation and filing for my work permit (OPT). These things are just standard administrative procedures, so there shouldn't be any issues. Graduation is around mid-May (the 15th or so), but I think I should be completely done with everything by early May. That said, I don’t plan on heading out to New York/New Jersey right away. For one thing, I need to wait for my actual diploma and work permit to arrive, and plus, I have a lot of friends in Austin, so it's a bit more fun here. I think I’ll wait until mid-to-late June before making the move. I don’t really want to sell my car, so I'm planning to drive it all the way out there—which means across half the country! It would be nice to find someone to join me on the trip and make the long drive more interesting. I’ll probably also go on a road trip with some friends sometime in mid-to-late May. Finding an apartment in New York or New Jersey is going to be a bit of a challenge. I may ask some friends to help me look, or I might stay with a friend for a while after I get there and search for a place myself. Either way, I'm sure everything will work out in the end, so please don't worry too much about it. Once I start working, the green card process will probably move rather slowly and may take several years. Because of that, the most practical thing would be for you to come and visit me instead. I think next year would be the perfect time. By then, I’ll be completely settled into my new environment, finances won't be a problem, and I’ll be able to take some time off to show you around. Please don't make any plans to dip into your savings for this trip. It simply wouldn't make much sense for you to spend your hard-earned money coming all this way. I'll take care of all the expenses related to your visit, so you won't need to worry about a thing. I’m enclosing some camera negatives with this letter, mostly photos of me and Sue. Sue is a friend I met through the International Office's PALS program (Partnerships to Advance Language Study). We meet once a week for language and cultural exchange. She’s in her late forties, originally from Germany but raised in the States, and she lived in Japan for five or six years. She just got back from Japan recently and has even traveled to China before, including Nanjing. She is an incredibly kind lady. Her father was a professor here at UT who passed away not too long ago. The photos were all taken here in Austin—some are at the State Capitol, and others are by the water at Town Lake. (I included a few shots of a swan; this swan was hilarious and so greedy for food that it almost swallowed my finger!) All in all, Austin really is a wonderful city. You definitely won’t find any swans like that in New York City. Love, Tong 4/5/97
