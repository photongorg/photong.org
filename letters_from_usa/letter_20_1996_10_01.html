The Trouble with Being a TA

Austin, TX
October 1, 1996
Dear Dad and Mom, I received your letter today. It just so happens that I've been busy all day, so writing back to you is a nice way to unwind. Being a TA is quite a hassle. I recently graded a project, and ever since the scores were posted, a lot of students have been hounding me. I’ve just had to keep explaining my grading and giving out a few extra points here and there to keep them happy, which has sucked up a lot of my time. Don't worry too much, though—it’s not always this chaotic. I had no idea sending an email was such a headache on your end, so I won't bother emailing you anymore. We'll just stick to writing traditional letters. As for the car, I haven't bought one yet; it's not an easy process. I’ll definitely be careful once I'm behind the wheel, so please don't fret. Out here, practically everyone drives—it's just like people riding bicycles back in China. The Certificate of Deposit for that money you gave me has already matured; I was still in New Jersey when it happened. The bank automatically rolled it over for another 10 months, which is fine since I don't anticipate needing that cash anytime soon. A few days ago, Weihai borrowed $3,500 from me to show as bank proof for his financial certification. He’s trying to get married so he can bring his fiancée over to the US, though I honestly have no clue how he’s planning to pull all of that off. Regarding what I mentioned about Robin in my last letter, it's enough for you just to know about her. Try not to overthink things or read too much into it. We're not putting labels on it yet. I'm finally learning that being in a relationship, even in this very early stages, takes a large amount of mental energy. Sometimes it feels kind of exhausting, and other times it feels great. I'll write more next time. I should probably head to bed now, since I'm supposed to meet with a professor tomorrow to discuss whether I want to do a Ph.D. under him. Nothing is set in stone yet, and I'll keep looking for regular jobs as well. Wishing you good health! Tong 10/01/1996
