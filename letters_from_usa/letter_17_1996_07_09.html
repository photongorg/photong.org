Mini United Nations

Eatontown, NJ
July 9, 1996
Dear Dad and Mom, I got your letter! I assume you've received the camera negatives I sent last time? I’m enclosing another batch with this letter. They were all taken in Philadelphia, New York, and Atlantic City. In the photos, that Indian-style building by the beach is a hotel and casino in Atlantic City called the Trump Taj Mahal. The Statue of Liberty in New York is on a little island, so you have to take a boat to get there—that’s why some of the pictures were shot on a boat. After messing around with the watch for a bit, I figured out how to shorten the band. It was actually pretty easy to pop a few links out. I’m wearing it right now; it looks great, and I really love it. Film isn’t expensive in the US—it’s only about two dollars a roll—so there's really no need to have someone bring any from China. I also don't want to hassle Weihai into bringing stuff back from China for me. He’s heading back to Beijing around August 10th and will go to Shanghai after that. I’m completely tied up this summer, but I don't have much going on for winter break, so I might actually be able to come home for a visit. A round-trip ticket shouldn't cost more than a thousand dollars. If I start working full-time next year, I'll have even less time to return to China. It’s just an idea at this point, let me know what both of you think. Work is going well. Mostly I'm just writing software. I'm responsible for completing a project. We get paid weekly; my take-home pay is $613 a week after taxes; before taxes it's $761. So you can see they hit you pretty hard with taxes here. I’ll be working for about 15 weeks, so I should make over $9,000 total (after tax). If I can bring $6,000 back to Austin, that will be pretty good, since my living expenses over these 15 weeks will add up. I actually just got another credit card here in New Jersey with a $5,000 limit. They must think I’m a permanent employee! It’ll be nice to use this card to pay for next semester's tuition. I'm so happy to hear that Dad's wind turbine design turned out so well. Keep up the great work! But please make sure to get some rest, too. It’s best to keep work and life separate—if you’re going to make money, you need to know how to enjoy it. At the very least, don’t skimp on your meals. I’ve become a pretty good cook lately, and someday when I see you I'll cook for both of you. Without a Ph.D. degree, it’s almost impossible to get a job at Bell Labs, unless you do consulting work like Jim. Consultants make better money, but they are essentially "temps"—not permanent employees—so it’s unstable. A lot of the Chinese guys at Bell Labs are consultants, whereas the permanent staff all have Ph.Ds. In my current department at Bell Labs, there are only two Americans; everyone else is from all over the world. We’ve got people from Mainland China, Taiwan, Malaysia, South Korea, India (a ton of them), Turkey, the Middle East, and Europe. It’s pretty cool, kind of like a mini United Nations. We got to try food from all over the world at the department picnic. I’ll stop here. Give my best to Wang Wei, Xiao Fan, and little Yueyue. Wishing you both good health, Tong 07/04/96
