Swim in the Delaware River (Not by Choice)

Eatontown, NJ
August 5, 1996
Dear Dad and Mom, I got your letter! I’m sure that previous letter I sent with the negatives isn't lost; it’s probably just moving a bit slow. It'll show up if you give it a little more time, just like that New Year’s card did. There are nearly thirty negatives in that envelope, mostly from my trip to Houston last year and my recent trip to Atlantic City. By the way, Atlantic City is a gambling town—it's not Atlanta, where they're hosting the Olympics. I have three weeks left before I head back to Austin. Work has been going really smoothly and I've made great progress; I finished writing two software programs. They actually want me to come back and work for another month over winter break. I haven’t given them a definitive answer yet because I don't know what my plans will be. We’ll just have to see how things play out. I might come home to visit you guys, or I might head back to Bell Labs. As for whether or not I'll pursue a Ph.D., I’m still on the fence. My last letter might have caused a bit of a misunderstanding. Generally speaking, a Ph.D. actually narrows your job options. Bell Labs is primarily a research institution, so naturally they value higher degrees and only hire international students who have a doctorate. However, most small-to-medium-sized companies prefer not to hire Ph.Ds. In other words, getting a Ph.D. often means you can only target major corporations or research labs. Next semester, I plan to take three more classes to see if any specific field catches my interest or if I can find a good advisor. I'll think carefully about this decision because it's an important one. Two weeks ago, a group of Chinese employees at AT&T organized a trip. About thirty people went, including me. It took about three or four hours to drive there. On the first day, we went camping, pitched tents (I actually forgot how to write the Chinese characters for "tent" and had to look them up in an English-Chinese dictionary—I'm starting to forget how to write Chinese characters sometimes!), slept in sleeping bags, and had an outdoor barbecue. The second day was canoeing. Jim and I shared a canoe, and we were supposed to float down the Delaware River for a full six hours. The river is very wide, and we were all wearing life jackets. Just twenty minutes after we started, our canoe flipped and both of us, along with all our food, went right into the water! We got completely soaked and had to swim about fifty meters to get to the riverbank. I ended up hitching a ride in someone else's canoe for the rest of the trip. I was cautious after that and managed to stay out of the water, but it was still a lot of fun. That was actually the first time I'd ever gone swimming in America, though not by choice! I keep joking with Jim that I saved his life because I pulled him out of the water. Last week, we went to the beach. It was pretty chilly, so I didn't go into the ocean. Before I leave New Jersey, I’m going to head into New York for dinner; someone is treating me. If it gets too hot in Nanjing, please make sure to turn on the air conditioning. Out here, you can find watermelon almost year-round, and it’s not too expensive. I've eaten quite a few, and they're enormous. I am eating very well in America, so don't worry. Lately, I'm trying to be mindful of eating less fatty food; otherwise, I’m going to start developing a belly. Are you guys watching the Olympics? They rarely broadcast any events featuring the Chinese team here. Tonight is the closing ceremony. I’ll stop here for now. Give my best to Wang Wei, Xiao Fan, and little Yueyue. Wishing you both good health, Tong 08/04/96
