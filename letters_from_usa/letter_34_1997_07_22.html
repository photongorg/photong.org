Goodbye Austin, Hello Edison!

Edison, NJ
July 22, 1997
Dear Dad and Mom, I’ve officially moved into my own apartment now! It’s actually pretty spacious. The neighborhood is really nice—it’s just a two-minute walk to a local park, and driving to a nearby mall only takes a few minutes, so it’s very convenient. There are also two huge Asian grocery stores nearby that carry just about every Chinese food item you could want. Edison has a large Chinese population. My new address is: 8 Koster Blvd. Apt. 3A Edison, NJ 08837 Telephone: 908-906-2487 This phone line is entirely my own, so you can call whenever you like without worrying about roommates. I hit the road from Austin on July 4th. My friend suddenly backed out a few days before the trip, but luckily I found another classmate who wanted to tag along to do some sightseeing and meet up with her friends in New York. Her name is Emma. The drive took seven days total, but we didn’t push ourselves too hard—especially toward the end, when we detoured to camp at a national forest and go swimming at the beach. We had a great time. I stayed in contact with Wei Jin along the way. He put a lot of effort into helping me with housing arrangements. I finally arrived in New Jersey on the night of July 11th and stayed at Wei Jin’s place for three nights before picking up my keys on the afternoon of the 14th. Everything went very smoothly. The car didn't give me any trouble at all during the trip, which clocked in at about 2,500 miles. And I drove about two-thirds of the distance. I took quite a few photos along the way—about 70 or so—and I’ll mail them to you in a couple of days. I even made sure to get the car in two of the shots. Since moving to Edison, I’ve bought a queen-sized bed, a dining table, and two chairs. I’ll pick up other things as I go, but the apartment at least has some furniture in it now. I’ve also been busy showing Emma around the area and helping her meet up with friends, which is why I’ve been running around the last few days and hadn't found a moment to write to you. I actually took a trip into New York City recently. I took the train in, which was quite nice and felt very safe—the conductors walk back and forth checking tickets constantly, so it feels way safer than the subway. The train ride from my place into NYC takes about 38 minutes. Three days ago, we visited West Point (where the U.S. Military Academy is), and the campus is absolutely beautiful. Yesterday and the day before, we went out to the Delaware River for kayaking, camping, and paintball. Paintball is a tactical combat game—not with real bullets, obviously, but with little capsules filled with colored dye that burst open on impact, splashing color all over you. It was a ton of fun. The paintballs travel really fast, though, so it actually stings quite a bit when it hits you! Today, we went to check out Princeton. Oh, by the way, the moving company already delivered my things, and everything arrived in perfect condition. I’ll wrap things up here; it’s about time for me to head to bed. Love, Tong 7/21/97
