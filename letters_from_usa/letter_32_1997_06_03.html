A 4,000-Mile Road Trip

Austin, TX
June 3, 1997
Dear Dad and Mom, I’ve been back from my road trip for a few days now. We hit a ton of places in just seven days—including the Painted Desert, the Petrified Forest, the Grand Canyon, Las Vegas, the Great Salt Lake, and Yellowstone National Park. The whole loop was over 4,000 miles, which is nearly 7,000 kilometers! There were three of us on the trip, and since we took turns driving, it wasn't too exhausting and we moved quickly. We had an absolute blast. We took plenty of photos, but I don’t have them on me yet since they’re with one of my friends. I’ll mail them to you next time. This trip was great practice for my upcoming drive to New York; I finally have some solid long-distance driving experience under my belt. As for the sights, the Painted Desert is an area where the terrain shows these beautiful, layered colors due to mineral deposits and weathering. The Petrified Forest doesn't actually have standing trees anymore; they’ve all mineralized into stone fossils and are scattered across the ground. We arrived at Las Vegas around 1:00 or 2:00 in the morning and spent two or three hours wandering through the casinos, which is right when the strip is at its liveliest. You have to be at least 21 to gamble over here, and they can never seem to guess the age of Asian people. An old lady at the cashier window where you can exchange money for chips asked how old I was. When I said 27, she just laughed and told me I was lying! The Grand Canyon was breathtaking; we camped out there and spent a whole day exploring. The Great Salt Lake is a massive saltwater lake with its own beaches and seagulls—if you weren't paying attention, you'd swear you were looking at the ocean, except there are no waves and it carries a bit of a strange odor. Yellowstone was our main destination. We camped there as well and spent a day checking out the sights, mostly geysers and waterfalls. Honestly, it wasn't quite as amazing as I had imagined, but all in all, it was a fantastic trip and gave us a great tour of the American West. My work permit (OPT) finally arrived, which is a weight off my shoulders since it’s the most important document. I also picked up my diploma—it’s just a big piece of paper with some text on it, no photo attached. My official start date at the company has been pushed back to August 4th. The firm wrote to me asking for my undergraduate and graduate transcripts. Luckily, I still had copies of my transcripts from colleges on hand, so I mailed those off along with my UT ones. I pulled off straight A's again this semester, which means my final cumulative GPA for the two years is a perfect 4.0. This year, Morgan Stanley hired about 240 Associates across 31 universities, splitting them into 9 different divisions. I’m assigned to Information Technology, while most of the other divisions lean heavily toward finance and mostly recruit straight out of business schools. We have an orientation scheduled from August 11th to the 15th at a hotel right on Broadway—coincidentally, the exact same hotel I stayed at during my interview. The orientation is mainly for us to get a breakdown of the company's business operations, and get to know one another. I’m going to hang out in Austin for a little over a month, mostly to wait for my classmate so we can drive up north together—plus there's really no point in heading up there so early anyway. My rent at my current place is only $160 a month, which is great, though the place is a bit of a mess because everyone living here is pretty chill. Lately, I've just been relaxing, watching TV, and reading. The TV setup here is fantastic; we get nine different movie channels that play movies around the clock. Just to clarify, the "New England" region generally refers to a few of the older states in the Northeast, including New York and New Jersey. Out of the Chinese classmates graduating with me this semester, two are heading to Washington D.C., two are off to California, two are moving to Houston, and then there’s me heading to New York. I'll stop here for now. All the best, Tong 6/3/97
