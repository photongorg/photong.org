How I Met Your Mother

Rockville, MD
Dear W. Austin and K. Joy, It must have been a sunny day—at least that’s how I remember Austin. In my mind, Austin summers are permanently bright, hot, and full of possibilities. I don't remember the exact date, but it must be my first week in Austin. I was walking down the stairs in front of the university library and trying to decide where to go for lunch. That was when I ran into this guy I had only known for a few days. Standing next to him was new student from China. Her name was Emma. She wanted to go to Dobie Center for lunch-there was a Wendy’s there. “Why don't you show her the way?” He asked. Now, that was an excellent suggestion, except for one small problem: I had absolutely no idea where Dobie Center was. So I said, "Sure!" We ended up having lunch together at the Wendy's. I ordered a simple burger. If I remember correctly, she ordered a complete meal with a baked potato. She carefully slit the potato open and topped it with butter, chives, cheese and bacon. She told me she had worked in sales at IBM China for a year before coming to Austin. She definitely looked more confident, capable and professional than I did. After all, up to that point I only had been a full-time student. She arrived in Austin at least a week earlier than I did and stayed at her sister’s place. "Why did you come only two days before classes started?" she asked, innocently. "Because every extra day costs some money that I don’t have.” I replied. Not exactly the kind of line you find in movies. Neither of us had the slightest clue what fate had planned for us. Dad June 18, 2026
