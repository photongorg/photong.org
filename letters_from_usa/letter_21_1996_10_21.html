'91 Toyota Corolla

Austin, TX
October 21, 1996
Dear Dad and Mom, I received your letter dated September 23rd. I’ve been pretty overwhelmed lately. On top of my own classes, I’m busy working as a TA—grading assignments, grading projects, and answering students' questions. Every time I finish grading a project, plenty of students complain about their scores and flood my inbox with emails. It is incredibly frustrating; at its worst, I’m getting over a dozen of these emails a day. On top of that, I’ve started looking for a job and have had a few interviews, all of which were on campus. No news yet, but typically companies require two rounds of interviews—one on campus and a second one on-site at the company. If I decide to graduate with just my Master’s degree, I’ll be finishing up next May. The bulk of interviewing will happen next year; this year is really just a chance to get some practice in. Robin and I are still hanging out and talking on the phone, but it looks like the relationship isn't really going any further. We may simply be too different from each other. I think we'll remain friends. I recently bought a car—a '91 Toyota Corolla sedan. It cost me $4,600, which is a lot of money up front, but relatively speaking I got a pretty good deal on it since the average market price is usually around $5,600. I should be able to resell it down the road, so it’s still a smart investment. I just bought insurance for it, which comes out to $640 a year. Honestly, it makes no sense to buy a cheaper car over here anyway, because the insurance ends up costing more than the car itself. I also got my learner's permit so I can start practicing driving. I haven't actually gotten behind the wheel yet, but I’ll probably start in the next week or two. I’ll be very careful, so please don't worry. Between the car and the insurance, my savings took a major hit, so coming home for winter break is pretty much out of the question now. Also, my roommate will probably be transferring at the end of the year to a school in Dallas, another city here in Texas. I’ll write more next time. Love, Tong 10/21/96
