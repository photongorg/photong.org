One Dollar Up

Eatontown, NJ
June 10, 1996
Dear Dad and Mom, This morning I went over to Wei Jin’s place for lunch and didn't end up leaving until about 4:00 in the afternoon. Jim drove me there. Their place is really nice. Their son is 11 now—he’s a chubby kid and really cute. His parents speak to him in Chinese, but he answers them in English. It’s pretty funny listening to them flip back and forth between the two languages. Uncle Wei and his wife seem to be in great spirits, though I don’t think they’ve completely gotten over their jet lag yet. I got the watch you sent with them. It’s great, though the band is a little too loose, so I’ll need to get it shortened. I also asked Uncle Wei to bring back some American coins for you, along with a few Canadian ones. Uncle Wei and his wife will probably stay here for over six months before heading back to China, health permitting. I’ve been at my job for a little over three weeks now. I get up at 8:00 AM to head into work, usually grab lunch at the office, and get back home in the evening. It’s an 8-hour workday, but nobody micro-manages you. It’s way more relaxed than a typical corporate job—honestly, it feels more like being at a university. My coworkers are incredibly nice, too. The workload is manageable, and I’m confident I can handle the projects. Working during the day means my evenings are actually free now, which is a nice change from my student days when I always had to read and do homework at night. On weekends, I try to get out and explore. Weihai and I took a day trip to Philadelphia and spent two days in New York; we hit a lot of sights and took quite a few photos. Last weekend, we went to Atlantic City to try our luck at the casinos. Atlantic City is a beach town, and it’s absolutely beautiful. I eventually walked away winning a single dollar—I came pretty close to losing money, but it was fun to experience the whole gambler's mentality, and we had a blast. Right now, I’m crashing in Jim’s living room. It’s huge but completely empty. We make dinner at home basically every night, taking turns cooking. All in all, I’m really happy with how work and life are going. The best time to call me is between 10:00 and 11:00 PM my time, since I'm almost always home then. With the 12-hour time difference, that would be around 11:00 AM for you. I got a letter from Yiqun three weeks ago, which is pretty much the only time I've heard from her since February. She was happy about getting her scholarship, and she said her parents were thrilled, too. She asked about the next steps for paperwork, and I’ve already written back to her. She’ll be attending the University of Maryland, which is right outside Washington, D.C. She emailed me a while back to inform me that Maryland offered her scholarship, but that UT was still her top choice and asked if I could look into her admission status here. I went to the department to ask around, but they said they never received her application files from the International Office. Apparently, applications go there first for processing before getting forwarded to the departments for review. When I asked what the hold-up was, they told me it was likely a "backlog," meaning a pile-up of paperwork they haven't gotten to yet. They weren’t even supposed to tell me that, since it's a private matter and third parties aren't allowed to nose around. Honestly, things between the two of us are pretty much a lost cause at this point, so please don't get your hopes up. I’m so excited to hear that Dad’s turbine design turned out so well! Is there any chance it can be marketed, mass-produced, or patented? By the way, I pulled off straight A's in my three classes again this semester. I’ll wrap this up here. Let's catch up more next time. Bye for now! Tong 06/09/96
