The Final Days in Austin

Austin, TX
June 30, 1997
Dear Dad and Mom, Time is just flying by—I’m leaving Austin before I know it. I’ve already coordinated with the moving company, and they are coming this Monday (the 30th) to pack everything up and ship it out to New Jersey. The cost will be covered by the company. Honestly, I don't even own any real furniture; it’s basically just a TV, a stereo system, some books, and some random staff. Wei Jin has already helped me find an apartment in Edison, New Jersey. It’s a one-bedroom for $695 a month. That’s just standard pricing for New Jersey, though it definitely feels pricey. If I wanted to live any closer to New York City, it would be even worse. The company already wired me a $3,500 relocation allowance. That’s the net take-home amount—the gross amount was actually $6,600, so you can see the taxes are incredibly steep over here. I plan to head up to New Jersey in early July. My lease there doesn't start until July 15th, so I'll just crash at Wei Jin’s place until then. I also formally submitted a "leave of absence" request to the department to officially pause my studies for a year. If it gets approved, it means I could theoretically come back and register for classes a year from now without having to re-apply for admission. In reality, I doubt I'll ever actually use it. Enclosed with this letter are some film negatives from photos taken during the Yellowstone trip. All the best, Love, Tong 6/28/97
