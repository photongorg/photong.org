The Girl I Met Online

Austin, TX
September 13, 1996
Dear Dad and Mom, I’m so sorry it’s taken me this long to write back—please forgive me. I’ve been back in Austin for about three weeks now, and everything is going really smoothly. I’m still living in the same apartment. I’m taking three classes this semester, and I'm also a TA for a graduate-level course. A lot of my friends are actually in my class now, which is pretty funny, though it might make grading a bit awkward. I received your very first email! I'm curious: since your computer at home isn't connected to the Internet, how exactly are you sending and receiving email? Can other people read your messages as well? If so, that doesn't sound very private. At least on my end, I'm the only one who can read my email, so you can write whatever you like. Email is amazingly fast—it only takes a few minutes for a message to get through—so we can use it for anything important or urgent, or you can just write to me whenever it's convenient. I get a ton of email these days. Yiqun actually emailed me a few days ago to let me know that she had arrived in the U.S. Weihai just got back from China a few days ago. He mentioned that if all goes well, he’ll head back around next May to get married. He stayed in Shanghai for 20 days but only spent a few days with his parents in Beijing—talk about a "undutiful son"! I really don't like that way of finding your loved one though. About two months ago, while I was still in New Jersey, I met an American girl online. Her name is Robin. At first, we exchanged emails almost every day, and she called me once. She’s a freshman here at UT Austin and, although she hasn’t declared a major yet, she’s very interested in psychology. She was born on November 28, 1977, which happened to be Thanksgiving that year. Her family lives in a small Texas town about a four-hour drive from Austin, on a farm that's about 150 acres. Her parents divorced when she was just a year old. She's really into sports and was on her high school varsity teams, competing in basketball, volleyball, softball, and several other sports—she's quite good at all of them. Since I returned to Austin, we've started hanging out in person. She has sandy-blonde hair. Her maternal grandfather is Native American, and her father is of Irish descent. She sports the unofficial uniform of a Texas college girl—shorts and a T-shirt—and has a refreshingly down-to-earth, unpretentious manner. We see each other quite often now—going out for dinner, renting movies to watch at home, chatting over coffee, and watching football games. We were supposed to go to a play tonight, but it got canceled, so we ended up walking around the mall and getting coffee afterward. We've been talking on the phone every day lately, and we get along very well. All in all, she’s a really nice girl. Don't assume all Americans are super open and progressive—she's actually fairly traditional and conservative. I really like her, and I think she feels the same way. We are simply seeing each other right now, and there's no way of knowing where things might lead. I don’t want to keep anything from you, but I also don’t want to raise your hopes and have you be disappointed if things don’t work out. I’ll leave it at that for now. Love, Tong 9/13/96
