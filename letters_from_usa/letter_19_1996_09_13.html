The Girl I Met Online

Austin, TX
September 13, 1996
Dear Dad and Mom, I’m so sorry it’s taken me this long to write back—please forgive me. I’ve been back in Austin for about three weeks now, and everything is going really smoothly. I’m still living in the same apartment. I’m taking three classes this semester, and I'm also a TA for a graduate-level course. A lot of my friends are actually in my class now, which is pretty funny, though it might make grading a bit awkward. I received your first-ever email! I’m curious, since your home computer isn't connected to the Internet, how exactly are you sending and receiving emails? Can other people see your emails too? If so, that’s not very private. But at least on my end, I'm the only one who can read my emails, so you can write whatever you like. Email is incredibly fast—it only takes a few minutes to get through—so we can use it for anything important or urgent, or you can just email me regularly whenever it’s convenient for you. I get a ton of emails every day now. Yiqun actually emailed me a few days ago to let me know she arrived in the US. Weihai just got back from China a few days ago. He mentioned that if all goes well, he’ll head back around next May to get married. He stayed in Shanghai for 20 days but only spent a few days with his parents in Beijing—talk about a "undutiful son"! I really don't like that way of finding your significant other though. About two months ago, while I was still in New Jersey, I met an American girl online. Her name is Robin. At first, we were emailing each other basically every day, and she called me once. She’s a freshman here at UT Austin; she hasn't declared a major yet, but she’s really into psychology. She was born on November 28, 1977, which happened to be Thanksgiving that year. Since I've been back in Austin, we've started hanging out in person. I really like her, and I think she feels the same way. We see each other pretty often now—going out for dinner, renting movies to watch at home, grabbing coffee, and watching football games. We were actually supposed to go see a play tonight, but it got canceled, so we went walking around the mall instead and got coffee afterward. We get along great and have been talking on the phone every day lately. Her family lives in a small Texas town about a four-hour drive from Austin. Her parents divorced when she was just a year old. Her family owns a farm that's about 150 acres. She's really into sports and was on her high school varsity teams, competing in basketball, volleyball, softball, and all sorts of things—she's quite good at all of them. Don't assume all Americans are super open and progressive—she's actually fairly traditional and conservative. All in all, she’s a really nice girl. We are just seeing each other right now, and there's no way to predict where things might lead. I don't want to keep anything from you, but I also don't want to get your hopes up and have you end up disappointed later. She is white, with sandy-blonde hair; her maternal grandfather is Native American, and her father has Irish heritage. I’ll leave it at that for now. Love, Tong 9/13/96
