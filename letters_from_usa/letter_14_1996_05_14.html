Destination: New Jersey

Austin, TX
May 14, 1996
Dear Dad and Mom, This semester is officially over, and my finals have been finished for a while now. I already found out that I got an A in the class I was most worried about, so I'm really relieved and quite happy. I leave for New Jersey the day after tomorrow on a 7:00 AM flight. The company is reimbursing me for the plane ticket, which was a little over $300 round-trip. When I land in Newark, my friend will be there to pick me up. He's the one who helped me with this job, and his name is Jim. I'll be staying in his living room. Weihai managed to find a summer job in New Jersey too—working for Siemens, a German company—so we'll be meeting up out there. Weihai is also planning to take a little over two weeks to head back to China; partly to see his parents, but also to go to Shanghai for blind dates. His parents set him up with two girls from Shanghai, though, he's never met them before. My job will mostly involve software development using C++. Conveniently, I was the TA for a C++ course this past semester. Honestly, I haven't been learning C++ for very long and have never used it to code a large project, so this will be a perfect opportunity to get some real practice. I'll be working 8 hours a day, 5 days a week. It looks like I’ll have to head into work pretty early in the morning, so sleeping in is out of the question now! But I'll have the weekends free to go out and explore, and New York City is very close. While I'm in New Jersey, my address will be: 66 Wedgewood Circle Eatontown, NJ 07724 U.S.A. Phone: 908-935-1168 I’ll give you a call within a day or two of getting there. I ended up dropping the price of my room to sublease it to a Chinese friend of my roommate's. Since so many students are trying to sublease their places for the summer, it's really hard to find renters, so I had to cut the price down to $230 a month. Still, it's much better than just leaving it unoccupied. I’ll be moving back into this same apartment when I return at the end of August. I'll wrap things up for now since I need to pack. A few days ago, I bought a small suitcase to pack my clothes and books for New Jersey; the two suitcases I brought over from China are just way too big for domestic travel. All the best! Tong 05/13/96
